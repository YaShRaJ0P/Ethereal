Power Saving Gadgets

We're a society of consumers, and energy is no exception. Our gadgets gobble more energy than ever before, but the average consumer doesn't really know how to conserve it beyond unplugging and powering down.

Why should you care? Beyond the obvious environmental impacts, energy-hungry devices could have an impact on your bank account. To estimate how much an appliance costs you to use daily, multiply the wattage of the device by hours per day the device is used, and divide that number by 1000:

(Wattage × hours used per day) ÷ 1000 = Daily kilowatt-hour (kWh) consumption

To determine how much that device is costing you per year, simply multiply this number by the number of days the device is used. Wattage of a device can usually be found on the back of most appliances, but as a baseline, most standard small appliances in the United States use 120 volts, while larger appliances use 240 volts. The Department of Energy also provides a helpful list of typical wattages of common devices.

1. Smart Power Strips:

These power strips are designed to save energy by cutting off the power to devices that are not in use. They have multiple outlets that can be controlled independently and can be controlled via a smartphone app or voice commands.

2. Smart LED Light Bulbs:

LED bulbs use significantly less energy than traditional incandescent bulbs and last longer, making them a great power-saving gadget. They are available in different color temperatures and can be controlled using a smartphone app or a voice assistant.

3. Energy Monitors:

: These devices help you monitor your energy usage and identify which devices are consuming the most power. They provide realtime data on energy consumption and can help you make informed decisions about how to reduce your energy usage.

4. Smart Thermostats:

These thermostats learn your heating and cooling preferences and adjust the temperature accordingly. They can be controlled using a smartphone app or a voice assistant and can help you save money on your energy bills.

5. Solar Chargers:

These chargers use solar energy to charge your devices, eliminating the need for a power outlet. They are portable and can be used to charge your smartphone, tablet, or other USB-powered devices.

6. Smart Power Banks:

: These power banks are designed to charge your devices efficiently, using less energy. They can be controlled using a smartphone app and can be set to charge your devices only when they are low on battery, reducing unnecessary energy consumption.

7. Motion Sensor Lights:

These lights turn on automatically when they detect motion and turn off when there is no motion, saving energy and providing convenience.

8. Smart power meters:

They monitor your electricity usage in real-time, allowing you to track your energy consumption and adjust your habits accordingly.

9. Energy-efficient air purifiers:

They use less energy than traditional air purifiers and help to clean the air in your home.

10. Solar-powered outdoor lights:

They use the power of the sun to light up your outdoor spaces, saving energy and reducing your carbon footprint.

11. Smart ceiling fans:

They can adjust their speed and direction automatically based on the temperature and humidity in your home, saving energy and providing comfort.

12. Smart power switches:

Smart power switches - They can turn devices on and off remotely and can be programmed to turn off after a set time, saving energy.

13. Smart cooking appliances:

They can automatically adjust their cooking temperature and time, saving energy and providing convenience.
